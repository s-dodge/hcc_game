STORY PLAN & OUTLINE
====================
Use this document to develop the narrative before writing in-game content.
Fill in the blanks, answer the questions, write rough prose — whatever helps.
Once complete, every room description and item should have a reason to exist.

PART 2: THE WORLD BEFORE YOU ARRIVED
================================================================================
The most important story is the one the player discovers, not experiences.
Something happened in this building before the player walked in.
Tell that story here in plain prose — doesn't have to be polished.

This backstory will never appear in the game directly. It lives in your head
(and here) so that every item and room description is evidence of it.

THE INTERN
----------
Name (optional, may never appear in game): Cade Hollis

Who were they?
(student worker, work-study, part-time — what was their deal?)
The "intern" is a student worker, they're 19 years old in their final semester at HCC. They are pretty normal, if quiet. Non-disruptive and good at their job, which is mostly imaging machines and replacing components for faculty/staff.

How did they first encounter it?
(what was the ordinary thing that turned out not to be?)
They found a copy of The King in Yellow at the local used bookstore. The book had hand written notes (some ideas, some footnotes, some questions, some references to Borges and other authors that may have had some insight into the unknown reaches of the human mind), as well as a few scraps of paper, one of which was a sheet of notebook paper with URLs for internet forums written on it. The forums were places for people with like ideas about the unknowable horror beneath the thin veil of reality, etc

What did they find?
(the USB drive? something in the library? what is on that drive, really?)
They found information - the dove deep into forums, articles, they dug into the twilit grotto's Esoteric Archives. They became obsessed with the intersection of technology, the infinite, labyrinths, eldritch gods

What did they do with what they found?
(did they investigate? run? try to tell someone? leave breadcrumbs?)
They become obsessed, and decided to transcribe some spells to disk instead of writing them down in a book or grimoire

What happened to them?
(are they gone? changed? still somewhere in the building?)
They were consumed by the edldritch horror, a victim of their own hubris. The echo of their conciousness is what is changing the school - It's trying to regain its body...by taking the player's

Are they a true believer, a victim, or someone who stumbled in?
True believer, zealot out of their depth

THE TICKET
----------
Who sent the "urgent password reset" ticket?
That's the weird thing - the Requester field is blank. That doesn't happen. The ticket was created by the intern via some eldritch SSH function

Is it genuine, a trap, or something else entirely?
It's a trap, intentional or no. The consciousness is only partial and may not have a true motivation other than trying to find a host - like water running downhill

Does the sender know what they're doing, or are they a victim too?
Both, sort of

PART 3: THE THING ITSELF
================================================================================
What is the eldritch presence? It doesn't need a name or a clear shape —
in fact it's scarier without one — but it needs a logic. What does it want?
How does it work? What are its rules?
The presence is an unnamed ancient entity - something that existed before god(s)

What is it?
(ancient entity, something summoned, something that was always here, other?)
An unknowable cosmic entity - not so much summoned. More like the way lightning will strike a rod, or the way water gathers at the lowest spot, or like celestial bodies drawn to other massive bodies

What does it want?
(consumption? worship? something stranger?)
At some point it discovered it could know or consider itself by consuming conciousness, so it is collecting minds/souls/conciousnesses
Cade's conciousness is still present in some way, if only the anxiety of not knowing what's happening. This anxiety may be what is transforming the environment. 

What are its rules / limits?
(what can it do? what can't it do? does it need something from the player?)
It can influence the world in small areas (like a college campus, for example). It can cause many worlds to coexist and overlap. It can reach out through electronic means now that Cade has written some spells to disk. It cannot directly manifest or touch/interact with the world

How does it manifest in the IT building vs. the library?
(same entity, different face — how does it express itself in each location?)
TBD


Does the intern matter to it, or were they just in the way?
The horror is wearing Cade's echo like a glove. It didn't become Cade— it picked him up.
Cade is still in there somewhere, just not driving. The thing knows the building, knows the
systems, knows how to generate a ServiceDesk ticket — not because it's intelligent, but
because it's wearing someone who was.

PART 4: THE PLAYER CHARACTER
================================================================================
The player doesn't need a name or a history, but they need a texture.
Even one detail makes the horror land harder.

Who are they in one sentence?
(tired, underpaid, been here 8 years, doesn't want to be here today)
They are just a helpful IT technician. They like their job, totally normal person.


What do they want at the start of the game?
(resolve the ticket and go home — this is the mundane goal the horror interrupts)
They just want to help


What do they stand to lose?
(sanity is mechanical — but what's the human version? what would it mean to
not make it out? is there anyone waiting for them?)
Their worldview, their conciousness, their relative innocense

PART 8: THE ENDINGS
================================================================================
The ending answers the premise question. Its emotional color is determined
by what the player knows and how much of themselves is left.

Think about 2-3 endings shaped by sanity + choices, not complex branching.

Example structure:
  - High sanity + right choices: escape with understanding. Costs something.
  - Low sanity: escape but you're not sure what you left behind.
  - Specific bad choice: don't escape at all.
